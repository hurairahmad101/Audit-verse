Root Cause — Why Tools Fail
Current tools were not built
for the modern audit function.
  • Spreadsheets: No version control, no audit trail, no concurrent editing. One corrupted file can invalidate months of work and create regulatory liability.
  • Legacy GRC platforms: Architected for checkbox compliance in the early 2000s. Rigid, expensive six-to-twelve month implementations, structurally incapable of real AI integration.
  • Point solutions: Separate vendors for planning, workpapers, and findings — no unified data model, no cross-module intelligence, no single source of truth.
  • Zero AI layer: Finding drafts, risk scoring, and framework mapping are still done manually — consuming thousands of senior auditor hours per year on low-value work.
  • No real-time visibility: Leadership sees the full risk picture only at quarter-end, months after issues have compounded and become material.
  • Framework library gaps: Most platforms cover eight to twelve frameworks. Organizations spanning multiple sectors or jurisdictions are left to manage the rest on their own.

Pillar 01 — Audit Universe & Planning
Know what to audit.
Before the regulator does.
  • Audit Universe: A living, dynamic catalog of every auditable entity across your organization — business units, IT systems, processes, vendors, regulatory obligations — with risk scores updated continuously based on new findings and environmental signals.
  • AI-Generated Annual Plans: Based on your risk universe, team headcount, and historical coverage data, AI generates a complete annual audit plan in minutes. Quarters mapped, entities assigned, coverage justified.
  • Coverage Gap Analysis: Automatic identification of entities that have not been audited in 12, 24, or 36+ months — surfaced before the regulator asks the question.

What this replaces

  • Manual risk assessment spreadsheets updated twice a year
  • Quarterly planning meetings with no underlying data model
  • Gut-feel prioritization of audit topics by senior leadership
  • Disconnected entity lists maintained across multiple tools
Pillar 02 — Engagements & Workpapers
Run every audit start to finish.
Without switching tools.
  • Full lifecycle management: Planning → Fieldwork → Reporting → Review → Close. Every stage tracked, every transition logged, every milestone timestamped automatically.
  • AI Fieldwork Guide: Enter the audit area and objectives — AI generates a complete set of test procedures and evidence requirements in minutes. Fully editable and version-controlled.
  • Budget vs. Actuals: Real-time tracking of planned versus actual hours for every engagement. Cost overruns are visible when they start — not after the final report is issued.
  • Three-level sign-off: Preparer → Reviewer → Lead. Each workpaper fully documented, dated, and digitally signed. Inviolable audit trail for every piece of evidence.
Pillar 03 — Findings & Action Management
Every finding. Every action.
Nothing falls through the cracks.
  • CCCE Methodology: Structured documentation of Condition, Criteria, Cause, and Effect for every finding. Every issue tells a complete, auditor-quality story suitable for board-level review.
  • AI Severity Calibration: Machine learning assesses severity against historical findings and regulatory precedent — reducing scoring inconsistency and bias across the audit team.
  • Multi-Level Escalation: Configurable thresholds route findings automatically to department heads, C-suite, or audit committee within minutes of identification.
  • Recurring Pattern Detection: AI identifies when new findings mirror previous issues — triggering root-cause analysis workflows automatically before patterns become systemic.
  • Management Action Plans: Owners are assigned, deadlines set, and progress tracked through to validated closure. No more open findings that drift for years.
  • Overdue Alerts: Automated escalation when action items pass their due date — eliminating the manual chasing that consumes senior audit team capacity.
